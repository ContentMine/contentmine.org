WikiFactMine - Wikimedian in Residence

Would you jump at the opportunity to be paid to help every human being share in the sum of all human knowledge?

The WikiFactMine Project, run by ContentMine Ltd, is looking to hire a Wikimedian in Residence to be partly based at the Betty and Gordon Moore Library, University of Cambridge.

We’re using machines to consume the academic literature and output facts that can be used by anyone. We want your help to make these facts as useful as possible on Wikipedia and Wikidata.

We’re mining thousands of facts a day from the Open scientific literature and we want to see these used to improve the free and open Wikimedia community. You don’t have to be an expert Wikimedian currently but you will be a good communicator who will thrive and make friends in a very diverse, global community.

We aim to use these facts to greatly increase the depth of information available and make sure that the information that is currently there comes from reliable sources. You’ll be helping to replace the famous “[citation needed]” with a reliable, verifiable citation and also to improve Wikidata: the free knowledge base.

Our facts are principally from the bioscience community and we focus on Open Access publications so a basic background in science and a keenness for sharing knowledge is important. Technical competency and computer literacy will be vital but no programming will be required.

You will also be an ambassador for text, data and content mining on- and offline. The work will involve in-person interactions with librarians, academics and members of the public in and around Cambridge.

You’ll organise workshops to both increase engagement with the Wikimedia community and improve the facts that we mine through communication with domain experts.

We’re a small not-for-profit with a team in Cambridge and across the world who are passionate about Open Knowledge, Open Science and really want to make an impact on liberating knowledge for the benefit of everyone. Come and join us!

The residency will last 6 months full-time and you’ll need to be able to spend around half of your time in Cambridge interacting with people in-person.

You can read the detailed Terms of Reference for the job below. To apply send a CV and covering letter to jobs@contentmine.org. For casual enquiries contact tom@contentmine.org.

Terms of Reference

Overview

ContentMine Ltd. is seeking a Wikimedian in Residence to work on the WikiFactMine project in collaboration with librarians at the Betty and Gordon Moore Library, University of Cambridge.

They will be working to promote content mining and Open Science and Open Knowledge to the Wikimedia community, members of the university and members of the public.

Specifically they will be developing workflows and techniques for including ContentMine’s facts extracted from the Open bioscience literature in Wikimedia projects like Wikipedia and Wikidata and working towards consensus within those communities as to the most effective way to do this.

The role will require at least partial time in Cambridge at one of the university libraries for in person meetings with librarians and to run workshops with academics and members of the public.

The successful applicant will be a full time employee of ContentMine Ltd on a six month contract.

Duties and responsibilities

  • Organise and run workshops to introduce librarians, academics and members of the public to the Wikimedia community. Help them start editing and contributing to projects like Wikipedia and Wikidata.
  • Coordinate with librarians to enable them to make contributions to Wikimedia projects relating to sourcing and verifiability. For example, adding citations and curating bibliographic data.
  • Work with the academic bioscience community to improve the coverage of the topic by Wikidata and use this increased coverage to improve ContentMine’s dictionaries and mining quality.
  • Outreach with members of the Wikimedia community online and develop session to improve the connectivity of Wikipedia, Wikidata and the scientific literature.
  • Work with the ContentMine development team to improve the quality of facts extracted from the literature by improving and helping maintain our Wikidata linked dictionaries.
  • Work with the ContentMine development team to create pipelines and workflows to migrate extracted facts onto Wikimedia projects. For example creating Wikidata statements referencing the source material or adding referenced text to Wikipedia articles
  • Create pipelines and workflows to improve referencing on Wikimedia projects. For example adding citations to Wikipedia articles, references to Wikidata statements where they are currently missing.
  • Work, in collaboration with librarians, on ensuring source metadata that the project uploads is consistent. Develop, with the community, standards for metadata about sources of various types and mapping between the data we upload and that used by more canonical bibliographic data models.
  • Monitor and track use of ContentMine facts and dictionaries across Wikimedia projects
  • Report regularly and on completion of the work, on the progress made such as events held etc..

You will be:

  • An excellent and patient communicator with a diverse range of people
  • Able to demonstrate excellent verbal and written communication skills
  • Passionate about disseminating knowledge openly
  • Scientifically literate (but not necessarily a scientist)
  • Experienced in organising workshops and meetings for both an internal and an external audience
  • Computer literate and demonstrate an aptitude for learning new software packages quickly
  • Able to develop and maintain reporting systems and evaluation.
  • Able to work tactfully, sensitively and effectively, as part of the institution, the Wikimedian community and with a wide range of individuals.
  • Able to prioritise workload and work under own initiative.

It would be excellent if you were:

  • A Wikimedian in good standing
  • Experienced in using Wikidata
  • Familiar with referencing, sourcing and verifiability

You can apply by contacting: jobs@contentmine.org with a CV and a covering letter explaining what you would bring to our dynamic community.
